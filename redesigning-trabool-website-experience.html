About the project

Trabool is a mobility and cultural leisure service in the Lyon area that can be used with a card. It is set up by the metropolis of Lyon with public and private partners. The Trabool site is used by users to order and recharge their cards and subscriptions from home, using a charger included in the kit.

As this service was deployed when I arrived in the Metropolis, I followed it for 12 months through several missions in parallel (graphics, user testing of the application, etc). I present one of the projects here.

Problem

As Trabool has evolved, new technical features have been added to the website to improve it: adding accounts, managing multiple accounts and subscriptions, ordering or renewing a card and many more. But some of these aspects blocked the user's journey through the site by the information architecture and layout. Led by the project manager, our team worked on redesigning the website and user journeys in order to integrate changes.

Solution

After several iterations and user tests, we have designed new interfaces for the registration process, the subscription and the management of personal information.

Process

My role

I accompanied the project during a work-study year, between the University of Bordeaux Montainge and the Metropolis of Lyon. My role in this project was that of service designer and ux, in a team composed of two project managers, a social psychologist and a UX designer, in collaboration with the team in charge of the website development (Sopra Steria) and a business analyst. Working as a team with Valentin and Alexandra, we decided together on the approach to be followed, alternating phases of work in co-design and alone.

1. Research

Â 

State of play

The project benefits from an experimentation phase led by Valentin Labit, a social psychologist, that is voluntary users known as "pioneers" test services before they are set up for the general public. They answer questionnaires and are mobilised for workshops. His expertise throughout the project and the information he has been collecting for several months has enabled us to understand the behaviour and emotions of users, identify problems and define their needs.

Defining the target

Trabool is a public service and is open to all inhabitants of the metropolis. The public of the Trabool service is all inhabitants from the age of 18. Currently, users are men and women between 40 and 60 years old, family and executive users who need to combine subscriptions and manage those of their relatives. Some services are still accessible to a particular target group.

Understanding the difficulties

As a result of its workshops and questionnaires, we were able to analyse that there were problems of understanding of the Trabool service on the part of the users as well as problems for :

  • ordering a card, viewing and navigating the available offers;
  • renewing a lost card;
  • transferring a partner subscription.

Many of the problems were due to the layout of the information, the prioritization of the information which caused a lack of clarity in the service and the use of the website.

Define design goals

During brainstorming, our team determined the main objectives of the roadmap according to the future stages of the project. We came to dedicate ourselves to facilitating the ordering of a card, the management of subscriptions, personal information and the overall organisation of the site.

For this mission, my resource was:

  • propose new, clearer and more intuitive user journeys;
  • reorganise the existing functionalities;
  • complete the graphic charter.

But in addition to the website, we worked on clarifying the value proposition of Trabool. I believe that a clear and strong service proposition is more likely to create engaging services. This would increase the subscription to services for recurring use. Most users use the service to renew their subscriptions, but do not subscribe to additional services.

We have also established a business model canvas of the current status of the project, identifying the value propositions and targets of the project. You can find this project in the following Sprint Design page.

3. Evaluation

Â 

Prepare user tests

Testing is the best way to evaluate the user experience. We have conducted usability tests with users to validate new designs that address problems. Testing on a loyalty-based interface will also allow us to understand how users navigate, interact with the site and perceive the service. Several volunteer testers were chosen for the tests, which took place in an individual room, via a laptop with video and audio recording.

Protocol & objectives

The objectives were to validate the user experience and our design assumptions before going into production by evaluating the overall system, ergonomics, usability and general understanding of the Trabool service. To do this, I designed a test protocol based on usage scenarios to measure the tasks performed for the routes and functionalities using an observation grid. The scenarios are used to validate or not functionalities and user journey such as:

  • The creation of Grand Lyon Connect and Trabool accounts;
  • The order of a card for yourself, for my family;
  • Subscribing to an offer;
  • Management of the offers subscribed in my subscriptions;
  • Understanding of "Tribe" and service in general.

Perform the tests

During the test, we observe and take notes on how the testers interact with the site, how it navigates, what they say.

The tests are followed by an interview and end with the SUS (System Usability Scale) questionnaire measuring usability and site experience in order to identify the value of our website.

Understanding the Trabool service

During the first tests, the testers had difficulties understanding the Trabool service and its ecosystem: a website to subscribe and a card used and how it works. Others did not know where to look for information about Trabool and the project leaders. Descriptions are not understandable and long presentation texts are rarely read. For the presentation of offers, explanations are missing and the reading is not visible, which can result in a negative experience because it is not understandable.

Order a Trabool card

When ordering a card, the subscription transfer is not included, as users are wondering if there will be redundancy in the transportation cards. We have also noticed a misunderstanding of the wording when ordering the Trabool card such as "Tribe" or "Activate transport function". Once the card has been received, it allows you to subscribe to public transport. During the tests, 1 out of 4 users did not activate it. Finally, when a new user creates an account, the switching to the Grand Lyon Connect connection site is not understood, and raises the issue of the overall consistency of Metropolitan services.

User feedback

The tests allowed us to build on the negative points of the site's course in order to improve them and to make hypotheses on the next steps of the design. Certain aspects that we did not fear, such as when ordering a card, were not understood on the whole and allowed us to take a step back to improve the interfaces. The simplest elements are the management and ordering of offers. But overall, the value of the service provided for users is not very clear and does not seem to meet their expectations.

4. Final Design

Â 

High-fidelity integration

After a phase of iterations on the high-fidelity interfaces, we designed a final version of the interfaces. The final version of the interfaces was re-tested in order to validate our final interfaces. We had to limit our ideas to the technical possibilities and the demand. In one version, I would have liked to integrate a map of cultural and sports venues in combination with mobility possibilities and open routes. The improvements reorganize the pages, making the user journey more fluid and integrating the management of several accounts. We have integrated a sorting by type of domain of the offers and a section dedicated to the new services for a simple display, a graphic coherence to keep, an account and subscription management and the responsive.

Sharing of advances

We designed the high-fidelity mock-up on Figma so that all team members as well as front-end developers can access it and allow them to inspect the file and export the CSS components. The development was followed up with the business analyst, and via a ticket tracking table to ensure that the implementation was aligned with the interfaces we designed.

Conclusion

Overall in the final version, users find themselves in the paths, the creation of the account and the addition and management of several accounts makes the understanding of the service as a whole and for new users clearer. This redesign of the interfaces and user experience allowed us to start the new evolutions present on the roadmap, by adding a ticketing system. This would add an accentuated use of the map and the application to come. This project allowed me to understand the importance of testing models as early as possible, in order to be able to decide more quickly on uncertain aspects of the design, while meeting time limit requirements.
